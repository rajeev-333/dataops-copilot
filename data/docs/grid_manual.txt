POWER GRID MONITORING SYSTEM — OPERATIONAL MANUAL

SECTION 1: SENSOR THRESHOLDS AND ANOMALY DEFINITIONS

Voltage Anomaly:
A voltage reading is classified as ANOMALY when it exceeds 255V or drops below 200V.
Normal operating voltage range is 220V to 240V.
Voltage spikes above 260V indicate critical fault conditions requiring immediate shutdown.

Current Anomaly:
Current readings above 20A or below 5A are flagged as anomalies.
Normal operating current is between 10A and 18A.

Frequency Anomaly:
Grid frequency must remain between 49.5 Hz and 50.5 Hz.
Deviations beyond this range indicate load imbalance.

Temperature Anomaly:
Sensor temperature above 60°C is classified as ANOMALY.
Normal operating temperature range is 30°C to 55°C.
Temperatures above 70°C trigger automatic sensor shutdown.
The temperature reading is in celsius. It's not in fahrenheit.

SECTION 2: SENSOR TYPES AND LOCATIONS

Voltage Monitor (Type: Voltage_Monitor):
Installed at Substation_A and Substation_D.
Measures AC voltage every hour. Reports to central SCADA system.

Current Monitor (Type: Current_Monitor):
Installed at Substation_B.
Measures load current and reports power factor every hour.

Frequency Monitor (Type: Frequency_Monitor):
Installed at Substation_C.
Tracks grid frequency stability and reports deviation events.

Temperature Monitor (Type: Temperature_Monitor):
Installed at Substation_E. Currently inactive (is_active = 0).
Was decommissioned due to repeated overheating faults.

SECTION 3: MAINTENANCE PROTOCOLS

Routine Maintenance:
All active sensors undergo maintenance every 90 days.
Maintenance involves calibration, firmware update, and physical inspection.

Emergency Protocol:
If 3 or more ANOMALY readings are recorded within 1 hour from the same sensor,
an emergency alert is raised and the sensor is isolated from the grid.

Data Retention:
Sensor readings are retained for 90 days in the primary database.
Archived data beyond 90 days is moved to cold storage.

SECTION 4: ESCALATION CONTACTS

Level 1 (NORMAL anomaly): Grid Operations Team — response within 4 hours.
Level 2 (voltage > 260V or temp > 70°C): Senior Engineer on-call — response within 30 minutes.
Level 3 (full substation fault): Emergency Response Team — response within 10 minutes.
